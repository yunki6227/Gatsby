Symbols from The Great Gatsby

  • Green Light-symbolizes hopes and dreams, especially that is hard to achieve like that of Gatsby's.
  • Valley of Ashes-symbolizes the consequences of the capitalist society, where if rich people exist, then the poor people must exist as well. It also represents how hard it is to escape their socioeconomic status that people are born in, because they are limited by what they can do in a poor society.
  • The eyes of the Doctor-symbolizes God who oversees everything people do, and that there is nothing people can hide and avoid consequences of.
  • Gatsby's mansion-symbolizes the hollowness of wealth and loneliness. Despite having a huge mansion and a large sum of money, Gatsby is struggles to get what he wants and feels lonely with only one friend, Nick, by his side.

    Themes from The Great Gatsby

  • Meaningless of the Upper Class: Despite Gatsby being very rich and throwing parties daily, he does not have anyone but Nick by his side. Just like people from the lower class, people from the upper class feels just as unhappy, lonely, and angry.
  • Social Class: In this novel, there are several occasions where social class influences things such as love.
  • The American Dream: This novel shows an example of Gatsby achieving the American Dream, yet he is never accepted by the upper class. The author signifies that no matter how much money you earn, it is not possible to truly enter the upper class.
  • Morality: There are many bad behaviors such as lying, cheating, etc. in this novel. However, no one seems to be punished by the law. The author demonstrates the problem where the money trumps the power of the law often in the real world with this. On the other hand, the readers can think whether or not it is okay to commit certain acts if they are not punished by the laws.
